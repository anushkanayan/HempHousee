INTRODUCTION

Industrial Hemp
Hemp, also popularly known as Industrial hemp or I.hemp, is a botanical category of Cannabis sativa that cultivars developed primarily for commercial or therapeutic purposes. It can be utilized to create a variety of items. Hemp is one of the plants on Earth with the highest growth rates, along with bamboo. Moreover, 50,000 years ago, it was one among the first plants to be woven into useful fabric. Many commercial products, including paper, rope, textiles, clothes, biodegradable plastics, paint, insulation, biofuel, food, and animal feed, can be made from it. It is a bast fiber containing cellulose, hemi cellulose, lignin, pectin. In India, the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act banned the production and sale of flowers and resin from cannabis. The problem is that hemp is related to the same plant family as marijuana. Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), a psychoactive compound found in marijuana, is derived from a female Sativa plant and is ingested to produce a "high." Conversely, hemp does not have the same effect because it only contains 0.3% THC where as in ‘marijuana’ the content is near about 15%.
