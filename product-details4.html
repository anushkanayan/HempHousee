INTRODUCTION

Sulfite Pulping Process involves mixing:
SO2(sulfur dioxide)+ aqueous solution of alkali= raw liquor for cooking wood.
In water:SO2+H2O= H2SO3(sulfurous acid).
It degrades and sulfonates the lignin by replacing a hydroxyl group with a sulfonate group allowing it to be solubilized and separated from the cellulose in non-precipitated form. White liquor is the liquor containing sodium hydroxide and sodium sulphide used to cook the wood chips. Wood chips are put in a digest or first and the white liquor is poured into it. In digesters, steam is introduced and lignin dissolves, wood chips break. Cooking liquid containing broken down lignin is called black liquor.
